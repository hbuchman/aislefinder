AisleFinder — Color Scheme Options

Four directions that keep the green identity. Every option softens the dark mode: the background gets a deliberate tint (forest or warm charcoal) instead of neutral near-black, the accent green is desaturated so it doesn't glow, and big headings use calm mint or cream instead of bright green.

Current (for comparison)

Reference

Pure white light mode with lemon amber; dark mode is neutral near-black with a saturated neon green (#3fd07f) — the combination that tends to read harsh and "terminal-like."

Option 1 — Warm Cream & Forest

Warm / organic

Light mode swaps stark white for a warm cream page with white cards floating on it, and the amber warms up from lemon to honey. Dark mode is a deep forest green instead of gray — the whole screen feels intentionally green rather than "black app with green buttons." Text is warm off-white (cream) to match.

Option 2 — Evergreen Chrome

Most distinctive

The top bar and bottom nav become solid forest green in both modes, giving the app a strong identity — the biggest visual change of the four. Body stays light and calm; amber becomes the active-tab highlight against the green chrome. Dark mode keeps the green header on a soft dark body, so it never reads as flat black.

Option 3 — Sage & Stone

Calmest

Everything desaturates a notch: sage-washed light background, a quieter garden green (#3e9d63) instead of the bright Kroger-esque green, muted amber. Dark mode is a warm charcoal with a soft matte mint accent — the gentlest dark mode of the four, good if the current one feels garish.

Option 4 — Market Fresh

Boldest green

Leans into green harder: mint-tinted cards on white, and aisle headers become filled green pills so the structure of the sorted list pops. Dark mode is deep evergreen with pill headers in a muted forest — the green does the organizing, not just the accenting.
