AisleFinder Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 19, 2026

AisleFinder organizes your grocery list by store aisle. This page describes what information the app handles and where it goes.

What we collect

  • Your grocery lists — the item names you type.
  • ZIP code — only when you search for a nearby store.
  • Email address and password — only if you create an account for list syncing and sharing. Passwords are handled by AWS Cognito; we never see or store them ourselves.

How it's used

  • Item names and your chosen store are sent to the Kroger API to look up products, categories, and aisle locations. Your ZIP code is sent to the same API to find nearby stores. Kroger's handling of that data is covered by Kroger's privacy policy.
  • Without an account, your lists are stored only on your device.
  • With an account, your lists are also stored on our servers (AWS) so they sync across your devices and can be shared with people you give a share code to. Anyone who joins a list with its share code can see and edit that list's contents.

What we don't do

  • No ads, and no selling or renting your data to anyone.
  • No third-party analytics or tracking.
  • We don't share your data with anyone beyond the services named above (Kroger for lookups, AWS for accounts and list storage).

Deleting your data

Deleting a list in the app removes it from your device and, if you're signed in, from our servers. To delete your account and everything tied to it, open an issue at github.com/hbuchman/aislefinder/issues or contact the maintainer, and we'll remove it.

Changes

If this policy changes, the update will appear on this page with a new "last updated" date.
